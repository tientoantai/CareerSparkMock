Narrow Your Search

  • Skills-based resume complete? Check. Now it’s time to zero in and find the job that fits you best. When it comes to your job search strategy, a focused approach is ideal. Concentrate your efforts on potential employers within your general areas of expertise.
  • You’ll save your time and your sanity. Not to mention, a focused search strategy results in more job offers and the best job offers. But don’t forget, a bank may be looking for an event planner or an insurance company may be hiring a nurse.

Read between the lines.

When it comes to deciphering job postings, sometimes you have to read between the lines. Here is a translation of some popular job posting terms:

Translating what they want.

Required skills vs. preferred skills

Required skills – The skills you must have to apply. If you don’t have ’em, move on to the next! Preferred skills – These are extra skills, the nice-to-haves. Companies may use them to decide between two comparable candidates

Willing to work flexible hours

This means you probably shouldn’t count on working 40 hours a week. Or that you should be willing to work overtime. It could even mean that regular work hours fall outside of the normal 9–5 schedule. Think hotel and hospital hours.

Entrepreneurial spirit

What a company means by this is they’re looking for someone who’s proactive, doesn’t wait for instructions, someone who’s eager for hands-on work, who takes the bull by the horns.

Active external learner

This company expects you to continue learning beyond your specific job duties. You should be willing to attend seminars, trade shows or even local college. This person aims to continually acquire new skills, not just settle for doing things the old-fashioned way.

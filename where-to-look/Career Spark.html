Diversify Your Search

  • You’ve got your skills-based resume and focused search strategy in place; now let the search begin. While a focused search is best, you also want to diversify. Formal channels, such as recruitment advertisements, search firms, on-campus placement offices and employment agencies, can be very helpful.
  • However, don’t underestimate the power of your personal and professional contacts. These are friends, relatives, and current and former colleagues and teachers. Using these contacts, combined with the more traditional channels, greatly increases your chances of finding a great job.

When it comes to applying for jobs, more is more.

Why you should diversify

If you really want to land the right job, applying for five positions probably won’t get you to Employee of the Month as quickly as you would like. More is more, as long as the positions you’re applying for are within your focused search and not all over the board. The more job postings you respond to, the more positions you apply for, and the more leads you follow will increase the likelihood of receiving that great job offer and reaching your goal.

In addition, think about beginning your search close to home. Start within a 10-mile radius, then expand outward as your job search evolves and progresses.

Searching for jobs online can be
a very effective resource.

It allows you to cover a lot of ground quickly. Literally, millions of job listings from thousands of websites can be found on some of these popular, tried and true job search sites. These sites are a great place to start, especially since you want to cast a wide net. (But don’t forget to engage ALL of your search tools for highest chances of success!)

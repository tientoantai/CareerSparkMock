Make A Good First Impression

  • The clichés are true. First impressions matter. And it takes only 3–5 seconds for people to form theirs about you. Your apparel, demeanor and mannerisms are all part of your image. And your image has the power to influence whether a potential employer
  • will consider you for hire. It’s important to
    dress professionally for any job interview, regardless of the work environment. The spectrum of appropriate attire ranges from formal to casual, depending on the position you’re applying for.

What are you waiting for? Let’s learn about snappy dressing.

Formal Interview

As buttoned up as it gets. Formal business attire is appropriate to wear in a professional office environment. And no matter your gender, this involves a dark suit. Gray, navy or black will do the trick.

Examples of places for formal wear: law firm, accounting office, or hotel industry.

What to wear for:

  • Women
  • Men
  • Dark Suit
    Light-colored Blouse
    Mid-heeled, Closed-toe Pump
    Portfolio or Briefcase
  • Long sleve shirt
    Conservative tie
    Professional shoes
    Portfolio or Briefcase

Semi-Formal Interview

Not formal, but not your favorite jeans either. This ensemble is more relaxed than a formal suit, but still neat, professional and pulled together.

Examples of places for semi-formal wear: real estate, pharmaceutical rep, or restaurant industry.

What to wear for:

  • Women
  • Men
  • Flattering Dress with jacket
    Tasteful accessories
    Closed-toe, low heels or boots
    Messenger bag or purse
  • Patterned shirt, professional tie
    Slacks or khakis
    paired with a jacket or sport coat
    Messenger bag

Casual Interview

Be casual, but keep it conservative. It’s a fine line because while you want to dress casually, you also want to dress smart!

Examples of places for casual wear: retail, advertising, or IT.

What to wear for:

  • Women
  • Men
  • Stylish blouse
    Slacks or very dark, tailored pants
    Matching accessories
    Mid-heeled wedge or boot
  • Plaid or oxford shirt
    Khakis or dark, well-fitting pants
    sweater or sweater vest
    Leather boots

General Interview Wardrobe

Do

Select apparel, jewelry, hairstyle, etc., that do not detract from your professional image. You want the interviewer’s attention on what you say and your qualifications, not your scent.

Cover visible tattoos to avoid distraction.

Wear clean and neatly pressed apparel.

Keep your look simple and successful until you become accustomed to the environment and learn about the company’s dress code.

Turn off your cellphone and keep it out of sight. Candy Crush Saga will have to wait!

Keep makeup natural and nail polish neutral. You want the focus on YOU, not your nail art.

Research how employees typically dress in your chosen industry.

Don’ts

Wear strong perfume. Fragrance can cause an allergic reaction or distract the interviewer. To avoid a fragrance fiasco, skip the perfume.

Wear transparent blouses, tight-fitting clothing, short skirts or low necklines.

Wear super-high heels or open-toed shoes. Stick to flats or low pumps in neutral colors, and save the stilettos for date night!

Leave in facial or body piercings. Single earrings are just fine.

Wear flashy or dangly earrings. Accessories should express your personality and accentuate your best features. But keep it simple!

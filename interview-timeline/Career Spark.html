Congratulations!

  • Your resume is complete, your search has been a success and you landed the interview. Remember to be positive and enthusiastic. Be yourself, but be the best version of yourself. Be mindful of your body language, sit straight, lean forward to show interest and make eye contact when you can. Feel confident in yourself
  • and your abilities, but never brag. You are here to sell yourself and your background as the perfect fit for the position, not to be arrogant. And please turn off your cell phone, or even better, just leave it in the car. Now let’s take a look at the before, during and after of a successful interview.

Interview Timeline

The Day Before

Verify the appointment. This shows forethought and allows you to make contact once more. During the call or within your email, you should:

  • Ask with whom you’ll be interviewing.
  • Verify the address and time of the interview.
  • Inquire about the length of the interview.
  • Ask about parking or traffic concerns if you’re unfamiliar with the area.

Prepare, so you’re not rushed or overwhelmed tomorrow:

  • Plan your outfit and make sure it’s comfortable, clean and pressed.
  • Lay out your portfolio, resumes and anything else the employer has requested. A notepad and pen are a must!
  • Learn a bit about the interviewers by checking their LinkedIn profiles and other social pages.
  • Know your own resume, review the company website and job posting. Plan talking points to show your understanding of the industry.
  • Plan your route to the interview, have a back-up route in mind, set your alarm and get a good night’s sleep!

Day of Interview

Listen to music or do whatever puts you in a good mood while getting ready. It will lower stress and let your true self shine.

Know yourself and be prepared:

  • If your mouth gets dry, bring a bottle of water.
  • If you sweat, bring a handkerchief to wipe your forehead.

Give yourself plenty of time to arrive 15 minutes early.

At the Interview

Stop, breathe. If it’s hot or rainy, take a moment to cool down or tidy up.

Enter the business with confidence and a smile. Everyone you meet could have a say in the hiring decision. And everyone you meet should be greeted with a firm handshake and a smile.

If you’re offered something to drink, accept their gesture of kindness.

If you got the meeting through a recommendation, show appreciation and say something nice about the recommender.

During the Interview

The average interview is 30 minutes long.

Use your talking points, but make sure they’re relevant to the question asked. Be courteous; never speak in slang or street lingo. And avoid military acronyms that your interviewer may not be familiar with.

Ask questions, leaving one or two for the end of the interview. This builds a two-way conversation and let’s the interviewer get to know you and how you think.

After the Interview

Show sincere interest in the position.

Ask what the remainder of the hiring process looks like and how long it might take.

End with another firm handshake, a thank you and a smile.

Later That Day

Thank each person you interviewed with. Sending a personal email to each one shows sincere appreciation and interest in the position, and a handwritten note takes it a step further.
